# Glioblastoma_RNAseq_and_Hi-C_Thesis
Code for thesis MSc project



Glioblastoma (GBM) cell lines are widely used as in vitro models of tumorigenesis, but their transcriptomic similarity to primary tumors and contribution of Polycomb group (PcG) proteins to this relationship remain incompletely defined. In this study, we analyze bulk and single‑cell RNA‑seq profiles to assess the degree of transcriptomic deviation and cellular composition between patient‑derived GBM tumors and their in vitro models. In addition, we analyze Hi‑C maps by examining contact scaling with genomic distance and PcG‑associated contacts, to characterize the chromatin architecture of GBM stem cells. Together, our results provide a systematic comparison of GBM tumors and cell line models and clarify the role of Polycomb proteins in shaping the transcriptional chromatin landscape of glioblastoma.
